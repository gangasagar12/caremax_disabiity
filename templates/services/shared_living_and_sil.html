Shared Living & SIL

Supporting Independent Living with Confidence, Choice, and Quality Care

About This Service

At CareMax Disability, we believe that everyone deserves the opportunity to live as independently as possible while receiving the right level of support to meet their individual needs. Our Shared Living & Supported Independent Living (SIL) services are designed to help participants build independence, develop life skills, and enjoy a safe and comfortable living environment.

Whether you are looking for shared accommodation with like-minded individuals or require ongoing support to live independently, our experienced team is committed to helping you achieve your goals while maintaining choice, control, and dignity.

Supported Independent Living (SIL) is an NDIS-funded support designed for participants who require assistance with daily living activities while living in shared or individual accommodation.

Our SIL services provide practical and personalized support that enables participants to develop essential life skills, manage daily routines, and participate actively in their communities.

We work closely with participants, families, and support networks to create a living arrangement that promotes independence, safety, wellbeing, and personal growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Supported Independent Living (SIL)?

SIL is an NDIS support designed for participants who require assistance with daily living activities while living independently or in shared accommodation.

Can I choose where I live?

We work closely with participants and their support networks to explore suitable accommodation and support arrangements based on individual preferences and needs.

Is SIL funded through the NDIS?

Participants with appropriate NDIS funding may be eligible for SIL supports. Our team can help you understand the funding and assessment process.

What level of support is available?

Support levels vary depending on individual needs and may range from a few hours of assistance each day to 24/7 support.

Can I live with other participants?

Yes. Shared living arrangements are available for participants who wish to live with others while receiving appropriate support.
